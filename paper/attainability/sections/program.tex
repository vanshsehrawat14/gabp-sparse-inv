\section{Limits and questions left open}
\label{sec:program}

The two probes resolve to attainability under the tested budgets, not
inductive bias among equally fit models. That conclusion is narrower than the
original hypothesis, and it does not turn the reference implementation into a
performance result. This section separates the paper's evidence from three
questions that remain legitimate but require new protocols.

\paragraph{What is established.}
On solve-shaped targets, a model containing the exact structured solve can
reach solutions that the tested finite Jacobi or Neumann alternatives do not
reach. The within-architecture interventions locate the difference in the
global solve or its backward, and the matched-fit read shows that the hard
cells are not equal-fit comparisons. The finite fp32 precision sweep provides
no robust accuracy advantage over a well-ordered dense factorization.

\paragraph{What is not established.}
The experiments do not show that sparse direct is uniformly faster or more
accurate than iterative methods; that exactness helps on targets not shaped by
the operator; that a finite Transformer cannot learn these functions with
different data, optimization, or architecture; or that the sparse-direct
backward has condition-independent error. The implementation is a PyTorch
reference, not a systems competitor to mature sparse-direct solvers.

\paragraph{Question 1: exact selected uncertainty.}
Selected inversion provides deterministic on-pattern covariance entries and
the log-determinant gradient. Whether that changes learned uncertainty
quantification is a separate causal question: the model, parameterization,
optimizer, data split, precision, and stopping rule must be held fixed while
only the exact versus approximate computation changes. CG/SLQ is the matched
computational comparison \cite{gardner2018gpytorch,ubaru2017fast};
variational or Vecchia methods change the statistical model and must be
reported separately \cite{hensman2013gaussian,katzfuss2021general}. A valid
study reports accuracy at matched wall time and memory, not ``exact'' as a
synonym for better.

\paragraph{Question 2: implicit gradients near difficult spectra.}
The four-cell DEQ diagnostic compares sparse direct with finite Neumann
truncations. A benchmark claim would require strong Krylov and preconditioned
solvers, phantom gradients \cite{geng2021training}, and accelerated fixed-point
methods \cite{anderson1965iterative,broyden1965class}, all scored at matched
gradient residual, memory, and time. The sparse-direct advantage is
iteration-independent work after factorization; its floating-point error still
depends on conditioning and factor growth.

\paragraph{Question 3: certified local versus global computation.}
For targets whose output depends on evidence at graph distance $d$, a strictly
local message-passing network needs at least $d$ layers for that evidence to
arrive. This does not imply a lower bound for Transformers or rewired graph
networks. A stronger future question is whether a local approximation can
carry a certificate that its downstream decision equals the exact global
answer, with exact fallback only when the certificate fails. Such a claim
would need proof, adversarial oracle checks, and comparisons with residual
stopping, adaptive message passing, graph rewiring, and graph Transformers
\cite{topping2022understanding,karhadkar2023fosr,rampasek2022recipe,
shirzad2023exphormer}.

\paragraph{Controls and evidence boundary.}
The first matched-fit HOLDOUT read passed the DEQ high-difficulty
attainability checks, but the DEQ low-difficulty relative-gap control failed;
its function-distance clause passed. For maze, the $K=256$ extrapolation-floor
comparison passed while the separately recorded in-distribution
\texttt{floor\_sound} diagnostic failed ($2.70\times10^{-5}$ mean loss gap,
$44\%$ of the exact evaluation loss, against a $5\%$ diagnostic). The
preregistration did not unambiguously choose between those two maze checks, so
NC2 is treated as ambiguous. The conservative result is therefore hedged. A
later reused-seed bootstrap branch is excluded from the evidentiary chain
(Appendix~\ref{app:integrity}). Any future task needs a non-solve-shaped
negative control: a persistent advantage there would identify a confound
rather than an exactness-specific mechanism.

\paragraph{Structural boundary.}
For later-neighbour counts $w_v$, the numeric storage scale is
$F=\sum_v(1+w_v)$ blocks while clique work is
$W=\sum_v(1+w_v^2)$. Exact sparse direct is attractive only when the fronts
remain manageable and reuse justifies factorization. If useful stiff problems
have fronts too large for direct factorization, or accurate iterative methods
reach the required tolerance more cheaply, the correct conclusion is that
exactness is unnecessary in that regime.
